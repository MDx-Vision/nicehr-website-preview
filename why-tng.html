The Standard Model

How Staffing Firms Operate.

The typical engagement path for healthcare IT staffing.

A health system contacts a national staffing firm for EHR consultants. The firm's sales team captures requirements. A recruiter searches databases for available talent. A consultant is identified, marked up, and submitted.

The sales representative has no EHR implementation experience. The consultant has no prior relationship with the firm. The health system pays for corporate overhead, sales commissions, and recruiter fees before any consulting work begins.

This model creates distance between the client and the expertise they need.

Standard Path:
Health System
    ↓
Sales Team (no EHR experience)
    ↓
Recruiter (database search)
    ↓
Consultant (no firm relationship)

Cost includes:
• Sales commission
• Corporate overhead
• Recruiter fees
• National marketing
• Consultant rate

Continuity

Established Relationships.

TNG consultants maintain ongoing relationships with hospital staff.

TNG consultants have worked with the same nurses, analysts, and IT directors across multiple projects and years. They know existing workflows, documented workarounds, and institutional history.

When a TNG consultant returns to a hospital, staff recognize them. Context does not need to be rebuilt. Ramp-up time is reduced.

Traditional staffing firms cannot replicate this. Their consultants change with each engagement. Institutional knowledge leaves when the contract ends.
